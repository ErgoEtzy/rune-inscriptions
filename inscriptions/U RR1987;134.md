## Normalization to Old West Norse

```text
"Ingilaug lét hôggva helli þessa eptir son sinn, "Sigfastr, ok "<-ihilt>, bróðir hans. Þ[a]u mœðgin búa í "Hú[s]um(?).
```

## Translation to English

Ingilaug had this rock-slab cut in memory of her son Sigfastr(?) and ... <-ihilt>, his brother. The mother and son, they live in Húsar(?)

## Translation to Swedish

Ingelög lät hugga denna häll efter sin son Sigfast, och  <-ihilt>, hans broder (lät också hugga). De, moder och son, bor i Husa(?).

## Other information

Salberger (i ANF 1989 s. 37ff) tolkar -ihilt som "Vigi el. Sigi let".
